Suttur Math

Ancient monastery dating back several centuries, known for its spiritual significance and architectural beauty. A center of learning and spiritual practices that has shaped the region's cultural landscape.

What's Special

  • • Traditional Vedic education center
  • • Ancient manuscripts collection
  • • Daily spiritual ceremonies

The Origin Sri Someshwara Temple

Sri Someshwara Temple is a historical temple built by King Rajaraja of the Chola dynasty. The deity of this temple is the incarnation of Shankara Narayana (the fusion of both Shiva and Vishnu). The deities of Amrutheshwari, Ganesha, Dakshina Murthy, Mahavishnu, Brahma, Chandikeshwara, Subramanya and Veerabhadra are also in the temple.

What's Special

  • •It is located within the premises of Sri Suttur Math.
  • • This temple was first renovated by H.H. Mantra Maharshi Jagadguru Sri Shivarathreeshwara Mahaswamiji in 1935.
  • • The temple was renovated for the second time by Sri Math in 2005.

Sri Narayanaswamy Temple

The Sri Narayanaswamy Temple at Suttur is a revered spiritual site dedicated to Lord Narayanaswamy. It holds great cultural and religious significance, with a rich history tied to the Hoysala dynasty.

What's Special

  • • The 4 feet idol of Narayanaswamy
  • • Earlier, this temple was in the middle of the village.
  • • was rebuilt by the Math in 2005

The Kapila River

The Kapila River is a significant river that flows through the Suttur region in Karnataka, India. It is considered sacred and holds cultural importance for the local communities. The river is part of the larger Kaveri Basin, and its waters are believed to have spiritual significance, with several temples and pilgrimage sites located along its banks. It is also a vital source of water for the agricultural activities in the area.

What's Special

  • • The Kapila River is also known for its role in agricultural irrigation in the region./li>
  • • Kapila River is a tributary of the Kaveri Rive
  • • The Kapila river is also known as Kabini

Gaddige

The Shrine (Gaddige) at Suttur is a sacred place. The shrines of H.H Jagadguru Sri Shivarathreeshwara Shivayogi and some other pontiffs of the Sri Math are located here.

What's Special

  • • It is believed that Sri Adijagadguru Sri Shivarathreeshwara Shiva yogi attained Jeeva Samadhi (achieve the state of God while living) here.
  • • This place is today an inspirational place for the devotees.
  • • Poojas are held at the shrine during Amavasya (The New Moon Day) and all special occasions.

Varuna Lake

Varuna Lake, located near Sutturu, is a serene and picturesque spot that has gained popularity as a hub for adventure and water sports enthusiasts. Managed by Outback Adventures, the lake offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and thrilling activities.

What's Special

  • • Scenic walking trails
