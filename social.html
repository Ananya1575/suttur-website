Social Services

Mysore Citizens’ Forum (MCF)

The citizens of Mysore have consistently extended support to victims of natural calamities, leading to the formation of the Mysore Citizens Forum (MCF) in 2004 to assist Tsunami victims. MCF operates as an informal agency comprising prominent citizens, philanthropists, donors, and voluntary organizations under the leadership of His Holiness Jagadguru Sri Shivarathri Deshikendra Mahaswamiji, Pontiff of Suttur Math and President of JSS Mahavidyapeetha. Its mission is to pool resources into a consolidated Mysore Fund for effective rehabilitation and disaster management. Following the 2004 tsunami, MCF provided immediate aid to the affected Modaliyarkuppam village in Tamil Nadu by distributing food, clothing, and essentials. They also gifted 30 boats to fishermen, repaired a school, and constructed 92 houses, later adding 160 more with government assistance. MCF has also extended help during earthquakes in Latur and Bhuj. Notably, all relief funds were entirely used for rehabilitation, with no deductions for personal expenses of the volunteers. MCF's initiatives reflect Mysore's collective commitment to humanitarian efforts.

MCF helps flood hit North Karnataka

The Mysore Citizens Forum (MCF) swiftly responded to the North Karnataka floods, sending 500 quintals of rice, medicines, clothes, and utensils worth ₹10 lakh under the leadership of former MLA M.K. Somashekar. Unused funds of ₹22 lakh from the 2004 tsunami relief were also utilized. Led by Sri Shivarathri Deshikendra Swamiji, MCF built 1,200 houses across five villages in Bijapur, Bagalkot, and Gadag districts. The Math also offered free education, boarding, and healthcare for affected children. A Padayatra led by Swamiji raised over ₹5.5 crore, with contributions from MUDA, Mysore City Corporation, and other donors, ensuring sustained support for flood victims.

Disaster Relief Through Unity

The Mysore Citizens Forum (MCF), with contributions from JSS MVP, Suttur Math, and Mysoreans, donated Rs. 10 lakh to flood victims in North Karnataka. Essential relief materials like food grains, medicines, and clothes were sent as immediate aid. Through a padayatra led by Sri Shivarathri Deshikendra Mahaswamiji, Rs. 1.40 crore was raised for flood relief. Additionally, MCF constructed 1,250 houses across five flood-affected villages in Bijapur, Bagalkot, and Gadag districts.

JHostels and Orphanages

In 1930, H.H. Jagadguru Dr. Sri Shivarathri Rajendra Mahaswamiji began providing accommodation and food to poor students at his rented residence, aiming to prevent them from discontinuing their education. This initiative led to the establishment of KSS Hostel, later expanded with a building donated by Sahukar Siddalingaiah. With blessings from His Holiness Jagadguru Manthra Maharshi Sri Shivarathreeshwara Mahaswamiji, a free hostel was started on Vanivilasa Road. Over time, the hostels grew, offering education support and lodging to thousands of students, including future leaders, ministers, and officers.
